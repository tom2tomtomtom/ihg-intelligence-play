⚡ Critical: MEA is Closing — A Vacuum IHG Can Fill
Meetings & Events Australia (MEA), the industry's primary independent professional body for 50 years, announced closure in 2025 due to unsustainable finances. This creates a significant vacuum in the Australian events community for education, awards, networking, and advocacy. ABEA (launched July 2023) is now the primary peak body. For IHG, MEA's closure represents both disruption and opportunity — to step into the community space left behind through thought leadership content, planner education, or awards partnership.
📊 How Australian Planners Choose Venues (Cvent Report)
96% of planners spend the majority of their budget on quality venues. Top selection factors: hotel professionalism (36%), venue cost (33%), booking experience (20%).

Loyalty insight: 71% of planners expect cost savings of at least 6% before switching from their preferred venue, and nearly 50% require 10%+ savings — demonstrating strong venue loyalty when quality is right.

70% of planners work with external planning or destination organisations, including convention bureaux and online sourcing platforms like Cvent. Over 70% organise 11+ events per year.
🔄 Post-COVID Behavioural Shifts
81% of Australian businesses have hybrid/remote working (Corporate Traveller, August 2024). 55% are increasing in-person offsite events — social events, team building, strategy days. Events are now a strategic tool for cohesion in the hybrid-work era, not just information delivery.

Small meetings rising: 78% increase in small meetings expected globally in 2025. 73% of APAC organisers report downgrading to four-star or non-traditional venues due to cost inflation — pricing pressure is real.
✈️ Rising Airfares Favour IHG's Regional Portfolio
Australian business-class fares forecast: +7.8% YoY. Economy-class: +13.7%. Across APAC, fares are 20–30% higher than pre-pandemic. This pushes AU planners toward regional and short-haul destinations.

IHG benefit: Vietnam (18 hotels → 40+ pipeline), Thailand (45 hotels), Japan (IHG-ANA partnership), Singapore (InterContinental flagship), South Korea (Grand InterContinental Seoul Parnas adjacent to COEX). IHG's APAC portfolio directly captures the price-driven regional pivot.

The Campaign Manifesto
The strategic heart of the campaign — everything ladders back to this

We need to talk about corporate events.

Not the sanitised case studies. Not the glossy venue photos. The truth.

Your last team retreat cost more than most people's annual salary and achieved less than an honest one-on-one conversation. Your leadership summit had a motivational speaker who makes more per hour than your entire customer service team makes per month.

We've built an entire industry around avoiding difficult conversations by hosting expensive distractions.

The 8% of corporate events that actually work don't pretend problems are opportunities. They don't treat symptoms as solutions. They don't confuse expensive with effective.

They start with a simple question: What are we actually trying to fix?

Then they choose venues designed for that specific kind of breakthrough. Not Instagram moments. Real moments.

IHG has 20 brands because different problems need different environments. The conversation that saves your company might need a boardroom. Or a living room. Or a rooftop. Or complete isolation from everything familiar.

Stop pretending your corporate events work. Start admitting what actually needs fixing. Then choose the venue designed for that specific conversation.

Intelligence over instinct. Honesty over hospitality. Results over theatre.
